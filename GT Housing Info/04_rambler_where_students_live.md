The guide outlines four primary residential options for Georgia Tech students.

## On-Campus Housing

Georgia Tech offers dorm-style residence halls designed for freshman transition, featuring furnished rooms, communal bathrooms, and meal plans. Upper-level students can access apartment-style residence halls with private bedrooms and shared living spaces on a first-come, first-served basis. Greek housing represents another on-campus option, with 24% of students involved with Greek life at Georgia Tech, though only a fraction can live in fraternity or sorority houses.

## East Midtown

Located east of campus beyond the Fifth Street Bridge, this neighborhood provides fully furnished student apartments featuring private bedrooms, along with traditional apartment options. Spring Street concentrates nearly a dozen complexes within a seven-minute walk of Tech Square, offering proximity to restaurants, cafes, and amenities.

## Home Park

Situated north of campus between Atlantic Station and Georgia Tech, this area offers rental houses through standard leases at potentially lower rental rates. However, many properties lack furniture and amenities, attracting primarily graduate students and upperclassmen.

## West Midtown

Beyond West Campus along Northside Drive and Marietta Street, this vibrant neighborhood features student and traditional apartments but requires more driving to restaurants and activities compared to East Midtown's walkability.
