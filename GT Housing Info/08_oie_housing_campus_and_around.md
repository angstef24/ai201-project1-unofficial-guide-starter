## On-Campus Housing

Georgia Tech provides various residential options through Housing and Residence Life. New first-year, transfer, and graduate students must be admitted before applying. Applications open in early spring through the My Housing portal, with an $80 non-refundable application fee required.

The institution offers one- and two-bedroom unfurnished apartments for graduate students and students with families. Room selection follows application order, so early submission provides more options.

Exchange students and visiting international students can access specialized housing. The institution offers two living learning community options, I-House and I-Lead, for visiting exchange and Techsplore students attending for 1–2 semesters. First-year degree-seeking students may join the Global Leadership Living Learning Community, which includes shared courses and residence in Matheson and Perry Halls.

Key requirement: The academic year housing contract includes both fall and spring semesters. There is NOT a fall-only housing application unless prior approval is received.

## Off-Campus Housing

Georgia Tech partnered with College Pads for an off-campus marketplace at offcampus.housing.gatech.edu. CORT provides furniture rental services at www.cort.com. Recommended search resources include apartment locator services, student organizations, and the GT Parking & Transportation website for shuttle information.

## Atlanta Neighborhoods

**Midtown** (closest to campus): High-rise apartments, condos, shopping, and Piedmont Park. Walking distance or short transit rides available.

**Home Park:** North of campus; features student housing in older homes at lower costs. Walkable/bikeable.

**Downtown:** Business district with hotels and attractions like the Georgia Aquarium and World of Coca-Cola. Less common for student housing.

**Westside:** Rapidly developing with restaurants and shops; accessible via car or apartment shuttles.

**Atlantic Station:** 1.6 km from campus; offers shopping and residential options; somewhat pricey.

**Virginia Highland:** East of Midtown; features affordable rents, restaurants, and entertainment venues.

**Buckhead:** 9.6 km away; upscale district requiring MARTA or car access.

**Vinings:** Quieter, family-oriented area with parks and restaurants.

## Temporary Housing

The Office of International Education recommends arriving during the week before classes, which allows time for orientation and check-in. ISSS provides a hotel listing with price guides ($ to $$$$) and transit directions to campus. The nearest MARTA stations are North Avenue and Midtown; the Tech Trolley stops at Midtown Station, and GA Tech is about a 13-minute walk from the North Ave. Station. Notable nearby hotels: Hampton Inn–Georgia Tech (4-minute walk), Georgia Tech Hotel & Conference Center (15-minute walk), and various MARTA-accessible options ranging from $50–$150+ per night.
