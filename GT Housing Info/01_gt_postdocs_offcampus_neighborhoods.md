Georgia Tech houses over 8,000 students on campus, leaving approximately 15,000 others to seek off-campus accommodations. The guide emphasizes that students should carefully evaluate housing options by considering roommates, utilities, monthly expenses, commute times, and parking fees.

## Student-Oriented Apartments

Several apartment complexes near campus cater specifically to student living with individual leases while allowing shared spaces. Notable options include The Standard, Square on Fifth (SQ5), The Mark, UHouse, and 935M.

## Neighborhood Descriptions

**Home Park:** Located north of campus, this neighborhood features a large student population and affordable living, with nearby dining favorites like Antico Pizza and Sublime Doughnuts. The Safe Ride Home program provides evening transportation assistance.

**Downtown / Centennial Place:** Close enough for walking or biking to campus, this area offers proximity to campus and attractions including the Georgia Aquarium and World of Coca-Cola.

**Midtown:** Georgia Tech sits within Midtown, making it walkable. The neighborhood provides Piedmont Park and its abundance of trails, including the BeltLine, and convenient MARTA access.

**Westside:** A rapidly developing area offering apartment complexes and small homes with emerging restaurants and shops, plus improved pedestrian access via the Path Parkway.

**Atlantic Station:** An outdoor shopping and residential destination within walking distance of IKEA, movie theaters, and dining options, though generally pricier than alternatives.

**Virginia Highland:** East of Midtown, this neighborhood attracts young adults seeking lower rents and entertainment within walking distance, though campus commutes require alternative transportation.

**Buckhead:** An upscale district approximately six miles from campus, featuring high-end shopping and dining, and requiring MARTA or a car for commuting.

**Vinings:** A quieter village-style neighborhood appealing to families and younger working couples, with restaurants, parks, and weekend festivals.

## Decision-Making Framework

The guide provides a comprehensive checklist of housing considerations, including rent costs, utility inclusions, lease terms, parking availability, maintenance policies, and landlord accessibility.
