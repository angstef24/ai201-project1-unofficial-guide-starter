The article provides guidance for Georgia Tech students seeking off-campus housing.

## Why Students Choose Off-Campus Living

The piece notes that over 60% of upper-class university students nationwide seek off-campus housing by their third year. Students are drawn to independence, lifestyle flexibility, and proximity to major employers in Atlanta's corporate corridor.

## Neighborhood Options

1. **Midtown** — Most convenient, featuring modern high-rises and student-focused complexes.
2. **Home Park** — North of campus, popular for affordable single-family homes and duplexes within walking distance.
3. **Atlantic Station** — West of campus with modern apartments, retail, and shuttle services.
4. **West Midtown** — Contemporary lofts with vibrant food and arts scenes.
5. **Downtown Atlanta** — Farther out but offers competitive rent with MARTA access.

## Pricing

Midtown is referenced as pricey due to demand, with pricing varying by unit type (studios, one-, two-, and three-bedroom units). Home Park generally offers more affordable shared housing options.

## Additional Costs

Students should budget for utilities, internet, transportation, insurance, and deposits beyond base rent.

## Transportation

Options include walking, biking, Georgia Tech shuttles (Tech Trolley and Stinger routes), and MARTA transit.

## Safety Considerations

The guide recommends reviewing neighborhood crime data, inspecting building security features, and documenting maintenance concerns.

## Lease Types

Traditional fixed-term, by-the-room, joint-and-several, furnished/unfurnished, and subleasing arrangements are discussed.

## Scam Prevention

The article warns students to avoid unviewed rentals, unverified deposits, and unclear ownership documentation.
