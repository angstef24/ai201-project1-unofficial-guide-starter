This guide assists incoming Georgia Tech freshmen with housing decisions. Georgia Tech does not require first-year students to live on campus, which expands the available options.

## Housing Options

**On-Campus Housing:** Students can join the First-Year Experience Program, which includes residence halls, mandatory dining plans, and community bathrooms. Most dorms accommodate two students per room, though triples and quads exist. East campus is in proximity to Greek Life, the football stadium, and Tech Square, while west campus offers a quieter environment and is closer to the Campus Recreation Center.

**Off-Campus Housing:** These apartments provide greater autonomy regarding roommates, amenities, lease terms, and location within Midtown. Students gain access to private kitchens and can typically secure year-long leases rather than nine-month contracts.

## Costs

On-campus housing through the First-Year Experience Program costs approximately $1,488 monthly (combining housing and meal plan). Off-campus student apartments range from $1,200 to $2,500 per person monthly, while traditional apartments span $700 to $3,000+ monthly.

## Timeline & Roommate Selection

Students should begin housing searches immediately after acceptance. The Georgia Tech Housing Application (available January) determines room selection order. Both on- and off-campus options offer roommate-matching questionnaires or allow independent roommate selection through social media groups.
